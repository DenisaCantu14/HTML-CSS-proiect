Interesting Facts About How I Met Your Mother

  1. There are only 12 times throughout the entire series that Barney shows up without a suit.
  2. One of the final scenes from How I Met Your Mother in which the mother appears was filmed years ago and only a few people actually know who she is.
  3. Cobie Smulders is from Vancouver. Her character, Robin Scherbatsky, was originally supposed to be from Toronto, but this was changed to make her also from Vancouver.
  4. Most of the websites mentioned throughout series are real, such as http://www.stinsonbreastreduction.com/, http://www.goliathbank.com/, and http://www.puzzlesthebar.com/.
  5. The idea for the "Slap Bet" between Marshall and Barney came from Bays, who would make slap bets with his high school friends.
  6. The most-viewed episodes were the series finale and the Season 1 episode "The Pineapple Incident."
  7. Did you guys notice that since they got engaged in almost every episode Ribin been wearing a suit?!
  8. Jason Segel was Bays and Thomas's first choice for the role of Marshall. They were both huge fans of Freaks and Geeks.
  9. After the show ended Josh Radnor kept the blue french horn, Neil Patrick Harris got the Playbook and the gang’s MacLaren’s booth, and the three yellow umbrellas now belong to director Pamela Fryman, as well as creators Craig Thomas and Carter Bays.
  10. In the DVD commentary for the Pilot episode, Josh Radnor reveals that he is allergic to dogs, something the creators didn’t know until Radnor was cast. That may have played a part in the decision to get rid of Robin’s dogs in Season 2, when she and Radnor’s characters are dating.

Interesting Facts About Suits

  1. Louis Litt invented and even trademarks the term “Litt up,” which he explains means to dominate someone else through wit, power, and intelligence. Louis “litt up” 16 people in the first four seasons.
  2. Ever wonder why Harvey seems like such a natural lawyer? Gabriel Macht has had a lifetime to prepare since there are quite a few lawyers in his real-life family.
  3. A name ‘Specter’ is actually an anagram for the word ‘respect.’ We think that is so unbelievably perfect.
  4. Love flashback episodes? Don’t worry, there’s one in every season (well, except for the first one).
  5. We can thank Aaron Korsh for adding some serious #girlboss goals to the show. Jessica was originally supposed to be a male character.
  6. Suits lovers Jeff Malone and Jessica Pearson are played by D.B. Woodside and Gina Torres, who also played lovers on the TV series 24.
  7. Family members appear on the show from time to time. Professor Gerard is Stephen Macht, Gabriel’s father; Zoe Lawford is played by Jacinda Barrett, Gabriel’s wife; and Claire Bowden is played by Troian Bellisario, Patrick Adams’ fiancé

Interesting Facts About Prison Break

  1. In actual fact, Wentworth Miller had to sit in the make-up trailer for just(!) 4.5 hours to fake the body-art.
  2. He nailed the part of noble Scofield, but Miller was actually cast on Prison Break just one week before filming began.
  3. Of the show's 17 regular characters, only two – Veronica Donovan (Robin Tunney) and Sofia Lugo (Danay Garcia) – were never arrested.
  4. Sara Tancredi was meant to die in the first season, but fans took to Sarah Wayne Callies's character and her popularity saw her stick around for more episodes.
  5. Stacy Keach, who played Warden Henry Pope, prepared himself for this role by spending six months in a British prison, in which he was even jailed many years ago because of cocaine possession.
  6. The characters of the show are named after US Presidents. ‘T-Bag” Bagwell is named after Theodore Roosevelt, and Lincoln Burrows is named after Abraham Lincoln. The main character, Michael Scofield, is named after Edward Scofield, and Benjamin Miles, “C-Note” Franklin is named after Benjamin Franklin.
  7. Sara lived up to Season 3, when she was killed because she was pregnant. Once again, the actress’s popularity among Prison Break’s fans got her back in Season 4.
  8. Robert Knepper (T-Bag), states that he based the hairstyle of his character on his old science teacher, because he always had a “poofy-bit” that stuck up at the front.

Interesting Facts About Lucifer

  1. Initially, Tom Ellis tried an American accent for his role but didn’t like it
  2. Every episode of Lucifer has Unique name and In almost all episodes of Lucifer, the name of the episode is said by Lucifer himself
  3. The name of Lucifer’s bar is “Lux” which is Latin for light, as well as the prefix and etymology of “Lucifer” (the light bearer).
  4. If you have seen Supernatural then you must know that there is also Lucifer in that show. In season 11 Ep 10, the show’s version of Lucifer says that if he got out of his cage in hell he said he would move to LA and help solve the crime but the amazing thing is that episode aired before the Lucifer pilot.
  5. The book Chloe reads to Trixie is “Coraline” by Neil Gaiman. Lucifer is based on a character in Gaiman’s “Sandman” graphic novels.
  6. Lucifer is never seen smoking; he is shown with smoldering cigarettes in an ashtray beside him or putting a cigarette out, or about to light it, but not actually taking a drag.

Interesting Facts About Casa de Papel

  1. The song used several times in the series, “Bella Ciao,” is a partisan hymn, adapted from an old Italian country folk song from the latter part of the 19th century. It is used worldwide as an anti-fascist hymn of freedom and survival in hard times.
  2. Denver’s unique laugh was written into the script even before Jaime Lorente was cast in the role. The script simply called for “trashy laughter” and each actor who auditioned interpreted that differently.
  3. All of the robbers were originally meant to have terminal diseases and that would be the reason why they would unite to make the heist.
  4. Río (Miguel Herrán) is the youngest of the heist group at 19 years old. Herrán likewise was only 20 years old when filming began.
  5. The original title in Spanish is “La Casa de Papel” which translates in English to “The house of paper”, related to the fact that the series takes place in a place where paper money is stored and used to print money. Another title under consideration was “La Casa Di Carta” (House of Cards), but that was abandoned due to the possibility of it being confused with Netflix’s other hit series
  6. The outside of the building where the heist occurs isn’t the actual Royal Mint of Spain but in fact the Spanish National Research Council in Madrid. There was an attempt to record it at the mint, which was denied by the institution. The interior scenes were recorded in a studio. Also, the Bank of Spain did not allow the production crew to visit the real building to replicate it on set. The interior sets were mostly fictional.
